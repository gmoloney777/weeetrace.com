The challenge

WEEE compliance shouldn't depend on spreadsheets

Recycling operations handle regulated waste across multiple hand-offs, sites, and processing stages. The reporting burden is growing — and the tools haven't kept up.

Most operators rely on disconnected spreadsheets, paper manifests, and manual collation to satisfy audits. It works — until it doesn't. A single gap in documentation can put compliance status at risk.

Manual record-keeping — Spreadsheets and paper forms that are error-prone, hard to search, and fragile under audit scrutiny.
Gaps in chain-of-custody — Hand-offs between intake, storage, and processing aren't systematically recorded.
Audit preparation is labour-intensive — Hours spent collating records across systems to produce compliance reports.
Increasing regulatory pressure — WEEE Directive requirements are tightening, and auditors expect digital, verifiable evidence.
The solution

Capture at source. Prove at audit.

WEEETrace records operational events where they happen — on the facility floor — and transforms them into structured, time-stamped compliance records.

Audit-ready evidence

Generate defensible compliance records on demand — structured for regulators, customers, and internal quality teams. No manual collation.

Mobile-first capture

Designed for industrial environments. Works in warehouses with poor connectivity. Captures data at the point of handling — not hours later at a desk.

Chain-of-custody history

Every item or batch builds a verifiable timeline: intake, storage, processing, and output events — all time-stamped and linked.

How it works

Compliance alignment

Designed around the regulations you report against

WEEETrace structures data capture and reporting around the frameworks that govern WEEE handling — so audit outputs match what regulators expect to see.

We don't claim to make you compliant — that depends on your operations. We give you the evidence infrastructure to demonstrate it.

Category classification, collection targets, and recovery/recycling rate reporting aligned to Directive requirements.
National transpositions (Ireland, UK, EU)

Chain-of-custody records that support waste duty of care obligations and due diligence requirements.
